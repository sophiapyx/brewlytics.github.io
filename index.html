An Overview of Restaurants & Cafés in Downtown Vancouver

This project explores cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver. We look at how different cuisine types are distributed, where food businesses are located, and how the scene has changed over time.

Breaking it Down by Cuisine

Breaking it down by cuisine — chart coming soon

This visualization will show the share of different cuisine types in Downtown Vancouver. It gives a quick overview of which food styles are most common, and how diverse the dining scene is.

We will highlight major categories such as cafés, Japanese, Chinese, Italian, and others, so users can see both dominant and niche cuisines at a glance.

Cafés vs Restaurants

This chart compares the total number of cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver. It provides a simple overview of which type of business is more common in the area.

By contrasting these two categories, we can understand whether Downtown is more of a “coffee and snacks” neighborhood or a “full-meal dining” destination.

This section will show how many cafés and restaurants opened in each year between 2018 and 2023. It helps reveal growth, decline, and any major shifts in the Downtown dining scene.

The chart will use the business licence dataset to summarize openings and closures over time.

Where Cafés and Restaurants Are Located

Map of active and closed businesses — coming soon

This map will show the geographic distribution of cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver, focusing on the core V6E postal code area.

We plan to distinguish between active and closed businesses, so users can see which streets remain vibrant and where closures may be clustering over time.

How Long Do Cafés and Restaurants Survive?

Lifespan comparison — chart coming soon

This chart will compare the estimated lifespan of cafés and restaurants by looking at the time between licence issue and expiry dates.

By summarizing average or median lifespans, we can see which type of business tends to survive longer in Downtown Vancouver, and how volatile the dining scene may be.

Data source: City of Vancouver Open Data Portal — Business Licences 2013–2024
