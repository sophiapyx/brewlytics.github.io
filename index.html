An Overview of Restaurants & Cafés in Downtown Vancouver

This project visualizes how cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver have changed in recent years. It highlights yearly openings, closures, and major trends in the local dining scene.

Scroll down ↓

Map

This map shows the geographic distribution of cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver, focusing on the core V6E postal code area.

We distinguish between active and closed businesses, so users can see which streets remain vibrant and where closures may be clustering over time.

Cuisine Overview

This visualization shows the share of different cuisine types in Downtown Vancouver. It gives a quick overview of which food styles are most common, and how diverse the dining scene is.

Major categories such as cafés, Japanese, Chinese, and Italian are highlighted so users can see both dominant and niche cuisines at a glance.

Cafés vs Restaurants

This chart compares the total number of cafés and restaurants in Downtown Vancouver, providing a quick overview of which type of business is more common in the area.

By contrasting these two categories, users can see whether Downtown leans more toward a “coffee and snacks” environment or a “full-meal dining” destination.

Average Lifespan of a Restaurant

This chart estimates how long cafés and restaurants remain in business by measuring the time between licence issue and expiry dates.

Comparing average lifespans helps reveal which types of businesses tend to survive longer in Downtown Vancouver, and how stable or volatile the local dining scene may be over time.

Data source: City of Vancouver Open Data Portal — Business Licences 2013–2024
